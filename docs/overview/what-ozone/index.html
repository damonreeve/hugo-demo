What Ozone

What is Ozone and how does it work?

Ozone provides advertisers, publishers and their programmatic partners with a range of services that enable

  • advertisers to reach engaged audiences at scale in quiality contextual environments; and
  • publishers to monetise their content and audience from advertising

Described here is a high-level overview of the programmatic ecosystem and Ozone’s role.

Programmatic Advertising - The Helicoptor View

At it’s most simple level programmatic advertising involves publishers and advertisers interacting with advertising technology companies (we’ll call them Ad Partners), who operate as a middlemen or brokers in the process of buying and selling advertising inventory.

Types of services offered by AdTech companies

Ad Partners offer many different services, business models and many of these services overlap with each other. However, these services can be generally organised into three categories

  • technology: licensing of saas technology to advertisers and publishers
  • data and analytics: various data services that improve knowledge and understanding
  • mediation: services that facilitate the programmatic trading process

bearing in mind that a single Ad Partner can offer any/all of these services, separate and combined, with a variety of different economic models to support.

A bit more detail

This simplistic view needs a bit more detail to be accurate as there are variations that are important.
1. Direct Sales Publishers and Advertisers don’t only operate programmatically. Directly sold campaign between publishers and advertisers don’t require programmatic Ad Partners.
2. Agency trading desks and managed services Agencies and advertisers often employ the services of specialists to manage their programmatic advertising on their behalf. Internally within agency groups these specialists are called trading desks, and independent companies are called managed services.
3. Publisher Adserver The publisher adserver is an important technology in the progammatic ecosystem as this is often the technology service that makes the final decision over which advertiser will win a particular impression and have their advertisement displayed on the page.
4. Analytics and data providers Numerous data companies are involved in the process of buying/selling and analysing programmatic advertising and they have relationships with publishers, advertisers and their agencies as well as agency trading desks and managed services.

Real-time bidding and header bidding

A characteristic that identifies programmatic advertising as distinct from direct advertising is real-time bidding. This is where each ad impression becomes biddable in an auction.

When a publisher page is loaded in a users browser each ad unit on the page:

  1. the page sends a request for an ad to the publishers SSP Ad Partners who, acting as auctioneer, create an auction for the impression
  2. A ‘bid request’ is sent to each of their DSP partners to solicit bids
  3. Advertisers wanting to bid for the impression submit a price they are prepared to pay for the impression. Their DSP collates all the bids from their advertisers, selects the highest bidder, and
  4. The DSP then sends this bid back to the SSP as a ‘bid response’
  5. The publisher collects all the bid responses from each of their SSP partners and sends these to their adserver
  6. The adserver reviews all the bids alongside the direct advertisers that have available, selects a winning ad and sends this back to the page to display to the user.

This whole process end-to-end is measured in milliseconds and should take no longer than 1-2 seconds to complete.

Real-time bidding uses a standard protocol called OpenRTB (current version v2.5), an industry recognised standard for passing bid requests and bid responses between publishers, SSPs and DSPs.

Deal Types

There are different types of commercial agreement that can be established between publishers and advertisers in RTB.

Deal Type Description
Open Auction / Open Market (OMP) An impression is made available in an ‘open’ auction where the highest price bid will win the impression
Private MarketPlace (PMP) An impression is made available to a buyer on contractual terms pre-agreed with the publisher. If an impression qualifies for a PMP then a ‘deal_id’ is passed in the request that only an individual buyer will know what the deal_id terms are and is eligble to bid on the request under those terms. A buyer bidding on a PMP may still not win the impression if an OMP bid is higher.
Programmatic Guaranteed (PG) Where a buyer has provided a guarantee to the publisher to spend a minimum budget within a fixed period of time

How Ozone fits in

In simple terms Ozone provides an additional layer between the publisher and SSPs. This helps publishers control who they are working with and importantly what information they are sharing with these partners.

Ozone Platform - in a bit more detail

It’s helpful to dive in to a bit more detail to see the different relationships between the different Ad Partners, publishers, advertisers and Ozone and how Ozone Platform fits in.
1. Direct Sales Publishers and Advertisers don’t only operate programmatically. Directly sold campaign between publishers and advertisers don’t require programmatic Ad Partners
2. Agency trading desks and managed services Agencies and advertisers often employ the services of specialists to manage their programmatic advertising on their behalf. Internally within agency groups these specialists are called trading desks, and independent companies are called managed services
3. Publisher Adserver The publisher adserver is an important technology in the progammatic ecosystem as this is often the technology service that makes the final decision over which advertiser will win a particular impression and have their advertisement displayed on the page
4. Analytics and data providers Numerous data companies are involved in the process of buying/selling and analysing programmatic advertising and they have relationships with publishers, advertisers and their agencies as well as agency trading desks and managed services
5. Publisher Direct connections In some instances publishers continue to work directly with Ad Partners to monetise their ad impressions. This is generally with Ad Partner tags directly embedded on the publisher page
6. Ozone Platform In the remainder of cases Ozone acts as an intermediary between the publisher and their Ad Partners, adding a layer of configuration and control for the publisher. This service is called Ozone Platform

Ozone Audience

Ozone Audience is an advertising monetisation service built on top of Ozone Platform. Ozone Audience provides two primary functions:
5. Audience Segmentation Monitoring user behaviour and content consumption habits on publishers websites and organises users in to targetable audience segments
6. Monetisation Monetises these audience behaviours through directly selling to advertisers and their agencies as a managed service.

A Managed Service is where Ozone secures a direct contract with an advertiser to manage the delivery of a campaign on their behalf.

Advertiser Self-Serve - Ozone Deals

Ozone Deals are instances where the advertiser would like to manage their campaign on their own, and would like Ozone to make the audience targeting segments they have agreed to purchase avaiable to them with the impression.
7. Ozone Deals Ozone works with selected SSP Ad Partners to create a deal_id that the advertiser can then target in their chosen DSP
